The big nature day — now with a choose-your-morning. Option A walks Acadia's surf-pounded coast; Option B trades it for e-bikes on the car-free carriage roads. Both funnel to popovers at Jordan Pond and the Cadillac summit, then it's the highway south with a dockside lobster dinner en route. Long day, zero filler either way.

Option A — Coastal classic · the original plan: surf, cliffs, Thunder Hole

Ocean Path → Thunder Hole
Walk it from Sand Beach. Loudest 1–2 hrs before high tide.

Popovers from the outdoor stand and the classic Bubbles view from the lawn. The 3.3-mile loop got cut for schedule — no regrets, the view is the point.
Option B — Carriage roads by e-bike · trade Ocean Path for the best ride in New England
9:00a
E-bike pickup — Bar Harborreserve ahead
Several shops rent Class 1 e-bikes (the only class allowed on carriage roads) — Bar Harbor Bicycle Shop, Acadia SUP & E-Bike, Island Time. August fleets book out; reserve online days ahead.
9:30a
Eagle Lake → Jordan Pond carriage loop
Rockefeller's car-free crushed-gravel roads: Eagle Lake, stone bridges, forest. Roll up to Jordan Pond House by bike (~11:45) for the same popovers — no parking-lot scrum. ~10–14 mi, e-bikes make it easy.
12:45p
Return bikes, drive to Cadillac
Trade-off: no Ocean Path walk. If it stings, squeeze Thunder Hole + Sand Beach as ~20-min car stops after Cadillac (2:15–2:45) before the 3:00 departure. Note: this morning also claims the same tide window as a Bar Island crossing (low tide 10:48a) — can't do both.
Either way · the afternoon is fixed from here
1:30p
Cadillac Mountain summitreservation
Timed vehicle entry — $6 on recreation.gov, 30-min entry window, stay as long as you like. Highest point on the North Atlantic seaboard. Save the QR code offline — rangers scan it and cell service is spotty. (Sunset variant: the 7:30p slot catches sunset, at the cost of a ~11pm Freeport arrival.)
3:00p
Hit the road south
Friday's 9am breakfast in Cape Elizabeth means sleeping south tonight — trust us, it beats a 5:15am alarm.
5:30p
Dinner: Young's Lobster Pound, Belfast
Right on the route south — pick your lobster, eat on the deck. Then ~2 hrs on to Portland.
